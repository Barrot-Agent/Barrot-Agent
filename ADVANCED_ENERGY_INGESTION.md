**Concept**: Stack multiple cells with different bandgaps

**Advantages**:
- Theoretical limit: >85% (infinite junctions)
- Practical: 47%+ demonstrated
- Better spectral utilization
- Proven technology (space applications)

**Data to Ingest**:
- [ ] III-V semiconductor combinations (GaAs, InGaP, etc.)

- [ ] Perovskite-silicon tandems (commercial nearterm)

#### 4. Hot Carrier Solar Cells
**Concept**: Extract electrons before they thermalize (cool down)

**Advantages**:
- Theoretical limit: 85% (single junction)
- Reduces thermalization losses (biggest loss mechanism)
- No need for multiple junctions

**Challenges**:
- Extremely difficult to implement
- Need ultra-fast extraction or slow cooling
- No commercial devices yet

**Data to Ingest**:
- [ ] Hot carrier physics
- [ ] Energy selective contacts
- [ ] Quantum well absorbers
- [ ] Slow cooling mechanisms

#### 5. Intermediate Band Solar Cells
**Concept**: Introduce energy level within bandgap for sub-bandgap absorption

**Advantages**:
- Higher current without sacrificing voltage
- Single junction can absorb wider spectrum
- Theoretical limit: 63%

**Data to Ingest**:
- [ ] Quantum dot intermediate bands
- [ ] Material systems (InAs in GaAs, etc.)
- [ ] Demonstration cells and limitations
